Week 1 Cultural Post

My impression of the DBC experience is a positive one. I think the first part that Shereef talks about in the video is the most important part - where he says that the only way that the program worked from the beginning was that everyone walking in was there to improve themselves, make a change, and to vigorously challenge themselves to learn software development. The way that DBC is framed- as an experiment- where anything goes and everything is accepted, as long as you're attempting to grow as a developer/engineer, speaks a lot to me. If you know that everyone that goes into this program, is there to learn and become both a better person and software engineer, then you will be free to take chances, push yourself, and better yourself without a specific expectation of what you should get out of DBC.

Hand in hand witht that concept is this "creator mindset" or "kitchen mindset" that Shereef discusses. I've been stuck in the "consumer" or "table" mindset for a long time- I expect to put in X amount of value (dollars, time, etc.) and expect a product come out in return. My career path has also been like that - I've been in finance and business my whole life, and I was taught to look at things on a pure return basis. You put some $ in, and expect a return. My functions were not creative either- I didn't create anything as an Analyst or a Consultant. I just made reports and pretty presentations. I want to make things. I want to be a creator. I want to be in a Kitchen working with other cooks, working together, learning from more experience chefs but also being able to teach inexperienced cooks, and create great dishes and food. And I think I've changed my mindset to be more in this philosophy. In joining this program, I think it really is the culmination of this change in philosophy. I'm confident that I can succeed, but I know DBC isn't a magical formula. But I know I'll become a creator and become a better person.

As a result, I think I would really thrive in this sort of environment. Everyone is coming in with a hunger to learn, create, and become a software engineer. Everyone is making a big sacrifice for DBC. Software development for the most part, is a team building towards a goal. Along with what Shereef said about competitiveness, (it's really not in anyone's interest to be competitive) and a preference for pair-programming, I think it would be a great envrionment to become a beginner engineer, and I am excited to finally take a part in it.

Sure, I'm nervous. I know I won't like working potentially 80 hours a week (I've done that before in my previous job). I know I'll be scared about not being able to find a job, or even worse, maybe even failing out of the program. I know leaving a life of comfort in order to satiate some weird desire to become a software developer is probably not the "safe" thing to do. But I look forward to the challenge. I need a kick in the ass.

I also look forward to sharing anxieties and frustrations with others who are in the same war trench as I am; sleepless, afraid, but deteremined to succeed.
